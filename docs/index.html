What each number is teaching you

📊

SPY: A quick read on the broad U.S. stock market. Green usually means investors feel better about growth or earnings.

💻

QQQ: A tech-heavy signal. It reacts strongly to AI, interest rates, and growth expectations.

🥇

GLD: A safety signal. Gold can rise when investors are nervous or watching inflation.

🛢️

USO: An oil signal. Oil connects markets to inflation, supply shocks, and geopolitics.

₿

Bitcoin/Ethereum: Risk appetite signals. Crypto often shows whether investors are willing to speculate.

Mini market dictionary

Risk-on means investors are more willing to buy assets that can move up a lot, like stocks, tech, and crypto.

Risk-off means investors are more cautious. They may move toward safer assets like gold or cash.

Oil affects transportation, inflation, and company costs. It can move because of supply issues, wars, or demand changes.

QQQ is more concentrated in large tech companies, so it can react more strongly to rates, AI headlines, and growth expectations.
